    My reflection is based of what Jessica Clemens spoke about friday

    The discussion on technology in education reveals diverse perspectives on its impact and utility. In the English department, where the focus includes rhetoric and composition, digital media studies are increasingly relevant. Historically, rhetoric dates back to around 600 BC, while composition as a formal discipline began to evolve in the 1960s. The field has grown to encompass multimodal approaches—incorporating not just traditional alphanumeric text but also visual, oral, and auditory elements. This shift highlights the benefits of combining various modes of expression beyond the conventional essay format, which is often mistakenly viewed as a single-mode medium. The concept of multimodal expression emphasizes the richness of combining different modes, such as color-coded text or multimedia elements, which enhance communication and comprehension. As a writing center director, recognizing that expression is not limited to traditional models is crucial. It challenges the standard norms of textual presentation, encouraging exploration beyond conventional formats like Word documents. The advent of digital humanities, which began with early conversations about integrating computers into the humanities around 1966, reflects an ongoing evolution. The focus now is on leveraging digital tools to address digital problems, acknowledging that technology has always been a part of writing and cultural expression. "Digital natives," those who have grown up surrounded by technology, are particularly adept at navigating and critiquing these tools. This includes understanding "critical AI literacy," which is essential in an age where technology shapes communication and learning. The debate over whether physical or digital writing is superior is nuanced. Context plays a significant role, and while some argue that physical writing offers a richer sensory experience, digital writing provides advantages such as accessibility and integration with other digital tools. Assistive technologies also need to be part of this conversation to ensure equitable access for all students. Concerns about equity and access are paramount. There should be no blanket bans on technology that disadvantage learners. Personal opinions lean towards supporting technology use, especially when it addresses specific support needs. Digital literacy classes and discussions about technology's role in education aim to equip students with critical skills and navigate digital spaces more effectively. The impact of digital systems extends beyond the latest gadgets. Historical technologies, like typewriters, were once revolutionary, and writing itself has always been a form of technology. This perspective challenges the notion that newer technologies are inherently superior or that older methods are obsolete. Social media dynamics, such as those in parenting groups, illustrate the complexities of digital communication. Analyzing discourse in these spaces reveals how advertisements and rhetoric influence discussions on topics like breastfeeding versus bottle-feeding. This highlights the need for critical engagement with digital content. As a manager of an open-access journal, it's clear that digital platforms have democratized access to academic knowledge, reducing barriers imposed by traditional print journals. This shift allows more scholars to contribute and access information without navigating restrictive gatekeeping practices. In the context of video games, understanding their importance involves recognizing them as part of discourse communities, similar to academia. Video games foster unique communities and critical play, challenging the notion that writing and other intellectual activities must be inherently difficult or exclusive. The stigma surrounding digital writing and gaming is diminishing, offering a more inclusive perspective on participation in these fields. Overall, technology and digital media have transformed education and communication, broadening access and enriching the ways we express and interact with content.
