Because if you look at how Chatgpt is being used right now, I would say a lot of professors and teachers would be disappointed with all the ways that students use AI to do their assignments. You can see how many students were affected by it and put all of their hope into AI helping them. This was an interesting conundrum. Do I help her basically cheat on her assignment by telling her about another large language model or do I let my close friend fail her test again. I think most people would say objectively you should let her fail because it was her fault she did not study properly but I think what is also important to note is that she is at a disadvantage compared to me because I have more knowledge in technology. I know about Gemini so if I was to do what she was doing I would not be worried about ChatGPT going down and that gets at an interesting problem where we can already see glimpses of non-techy people being left behind in education. So back to my main argument of allowing technology in the classroom; does allowing tech in education influence the problem I addressed above. It’s complicated and I think we need to keep talking about it. What I am afraid of is we just pretend AI does not exist and we all end up like my friend, failing because we were not aware of tech being available.
