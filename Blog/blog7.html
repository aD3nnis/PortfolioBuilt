Data whose originator had bad intentions

Misleading political polls

So when data is shared can have an effect on election polls. In October 2016, just 11 days before the U.S. presidential election, then-FBI Director James Comey announced the reopening of the investigation into Hillary Clinton's private email server. This announcement dominated news cycles, and polls conducted in the immediate aftermath showed a sudden dip in Clinton's support.

Polls conducted in the days immediately following the announcement capitalized on the temporary shock and outrage among voters. So what was seen is there was a sharp decline in support for Clinton.
